[Studio / Brand Name] Style Guide · 02
Before Your Session

What to wear

The golden rule: coordinate, don't match. Pick a palette and let everyone pull from it — you'll look pulled-together without looking like a uniform. Start with one person's outfit, then build the rest of the family around those colors.

Build Your Palette
Choose 2–3 complementary colors plus a soft neutral — cream, tan, denim, sage, or grey. Earthy, muted tones photograph beautifully.
Add Texture & Layers
Knits, denim, linen, and lace add richness on camera. Cardigans, hats, and scarves give depth — and easy options mid-session.
Dressing the Little Ones
Comfortable kids = happy photos. Skip brand-new shoes and anything itchy. Bring a backup outfit for spills.
Season & Location
Dress for the setting and the weather — [e.g., warm layers for autumn fields, lighter linens for the beach]. Comfort reads as confidence.
A Few Things to Avoid
Large logos, cartoon characters, or bright neons that pull the eye.
All-white or all-black head to toe — it tends to flatten in photos.
Busy patterns on more than one person — pick one "statement" piece.

Get Ready

Session prep checklist

A little planning makes session day feel easy. Work through this in the week leading up, and arrive relaxed and ready.

One Week Before
Finalize and lay out everyone's outfits
Book any haircuts or trims (3–4 days ahead)
Confirm the date, time, and location
Send me any questions or must-have shots
The Day Before
Steam or iron outfits and set them aside
Get everyone to bed early
Hydrate — rested, watered skin photographs best
Pack snacks, wipes, and a favorite toy
The Morning Of
Eat a good meal; pack non-messy snacks
Bring water and any touch-up makeup
Wipe little faces and hands before we start
Arrive 5–10 minutes early to settle in
Leave at Home
Pressure for "perfect" — candid beats posed
Hair ties on wrists (they show in photos)
Temporary tattoos, face paint, and marker
