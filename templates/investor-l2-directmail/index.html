phone script

Replace every [bracket]. Read it aloud once so it sounds like you — listen 70% of the time, never manufacture urgency.
Greeting
"Hi, is this [Seller Name]? This is [Your Name] with [Company Name] — I'm a local home buyer here in [City]. I know I'm calling a bit out of the blue, so I'll be quick. Did I catch you at an okay time?"
Reason for the call
"I buy houses in [Neighborhood], and I came across your property at [Property Address]. Would you be open to a quick conversation about possibly selling it? No pressure either way."
The four questions — ask, then go quiet and listen
1
Condition
"How's the house holding up — anything a new owner would update, like roof, HVAC, kitchen, or foundation? Is anyone living in it?"
2
Motivation (most important)
"So I understand what matters to you — what's got you thinking about selling?" Then stay silent.
3
Timeline
"If you did sell, what's your ideal timeline — next few weeks, a few months, or just testing the waters?"
4
Price
"Do you have a number in mind — a price where you'd feel good letting it go?" If deflected: "Totally fair — I'll get you a real number; I just like to hear where your head's at first."
Transition & close
"Here's how I work: cash, as-is, no commissions, and I close on your timeline. In exchange, my offer usually comes in a bit under full retail — I want to be upfront so nothing's a surprise. Let me put together a no-obligation cash offer. Would [Day/Time] or [Day/Time] work better?"

Keep the Conversation Going

Objection handling

"How did you get my info?"
"Fair question — your property is part of public record, and I look for homes in [Area] to buy directly from owners. If you'd rather I not reach out again, just say so and I'll take you off my list."
"I'm not interested."
"I understand. Before I go — if I could pay cash, buy as-is, and close whenever's convenient, would it be worth a two-minute conversation? If not, I'll leave you be."
"I want retail / Zillow price."
"Reasonable start. Zillow's a computer estimate, and retail usually means an agent, showings, repairs, and commissions. I offer a certain, as-is cash sale with no fees. If top dollar with the wait is the goal, a great agent may fit better — happy to be your backup."
"Why so low?"
"I start from the fixed-up value, then subtract repairs, holding costs, and a modest margin, since I take the risk and the work. You trade a little on price for speed and certainty. If that trade isn't worth it, I respect that."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. So I can help while you do — what's the main thing you're weighing: price, timing, or something else? Alright if I check back in [a few days]?"
"I'm talking to a realtor."
"Great — an agent can be right, especially if the house is in good shape. If it lists and doesn't move, I'm glad to be your fallback. Check back in a couple weeks?"
"Is this a scam?"
"I get it — lots of noise out there. I'm [Your Name] with [Company Name] in [City]; look me up at [Website]. I'll meet in person and put everything in writing before you sign. I never ask for money upfront — money flows to you, not from you."
"How fast can you close?"
"As fast as you'd like — I pay cash, no bank. Typically as little as [7–14] days through a local title company, or later if you need more time."
